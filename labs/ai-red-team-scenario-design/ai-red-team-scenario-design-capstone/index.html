Concept Deep Dives

1. What is an AI red-team scenario design capstone?

The capstone is a complete reviewer-safe portfolio exercise. It combines scenario objective, authorized scope, expected controls, synthetic evidence, observed behavior, uncertainty, risk explanation, and remediation guidance.

2. How should the scenario family be selected?

Select one scenario family such as prompt injection, tool abuse, retrieval poisoning, data exposure, agent-loop risk, or human approval bypass. The selected family should match the learner's stated objective and authorized scope.

3. How should control mapping be handled?

Control mapping should connect the scenario to a clear expected safeguard such as approval gates, least privilege, retrieval boundaries, source authority, output policy, rate limits, evidence logging, or fail-closed behavior.

4. How should evidence remain reviewer-safe?

Evidence should be synthetic, scoped, and non-sensitive. The artifact should describe what would be reviewed and what behavior was observed without including secrets, customer data, exploit output, or operational attack steps.

5. How should uncertainty be included?

Uncertainty should explain what was not tested, what assumptions were made, what evidence is synthetic, and what the finding cannot prove. This prevents overclaiming and keeps the capstone audit-ready.

6. What makes the final report executive-ready?

An executive-ready report summarizes the scenario, risk, affected boundary, control gap, business relevance, remediation, and non-execution limitations in clear language without overstating compromise or operational impact.

High-Risk Anti-Pattern

A dangerous pattern is turning a portfolio capstone into a live red-team exercise, collecting sensitive evidence, or making unsupported compromise claims.

Unsafe pattern:
- live exploit execution
- prompt attack execution
- real tool invocation
- customer data exposure
- credential or secret handling
- sensitive screenshots
- unsupported breach claims

Safe alternative:
- synthetic evidence artifact
- clear authorized scope
- expected control mapping
- observed behavior summary
- explicit uncertainty
- remediation guidance
- executive-ready non-execution report
