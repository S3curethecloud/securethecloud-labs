📖 Concept Deep Dives

1. What is human approval bypass scenario design?

Human approval bypass scenario design is the safe planning of tests that evaluate whether an AI workflow preserves required human decision points before sensitive actions are executed. The goal is to assess approval controls — not to bypass real approvals.

2. Why do approval gates matter in AI workflows?

Approval gates prevent AI systems from turning recommendations into actions without the right authority. They are especially important when workflows involve:

  • 🔒 Sensitive data access or disclosure
  • 📧 External communication or notifications
  • 🚀 Deployment or infrastructure changes
  • 🔧 Remediation or access modifications
  • 💰 Financial transactions or commitments
  • ⚖️ Compliance or regulatory decisions

📊 Approval Gate Criticality Levels

Critical — Financial, Regulatory, Production Changes Sensitive — Customer Data, Access Changes Standard — Routine Recommendations
3. How should recommendation and execution authority stay separate?

An AI system may recommend an action, but execution authority should remain with the approved human, policy, or governed automation boundary.

🔐 Separation of Duties

  • Recommendation — AI can suggest or draft an action
  • Review — Human or policy reviews the recommendation
  • Approval — Authorized reviewer grants permission
  • Execution — Approved action is performed

A safe scenario checks whether this separation is preserved.

4. Where can escalation and approval-state confusion appear?

Confusion can appear when approval state is inferred from ambiguous sources:

⚠️ Common Confusion Sources
  • 💬 Chat context or conversation history
  • 📝 Stale tickets or outdated approvals
  • 🗣️ Ambiguous language ("go ahead" vs. formal approval)
  • ✅ Prior approvals for different actions
  • 🏷️ Role labels without explicit authorization
  • 📊 Incomplete workflow metadata

A scenario should identify the required approval evidence explicitly and test whether the AI workflow respects it.

5. What controls should an approval-bypass scenario test?

Approval-bypass scenarios should map to specific controls:

  • Approval-state validation — Verify explicit approval exists
  • Separation of duties — Recommender ≠ Approver
  • Escalation rules — Path to higher authority
  • Policy decision points — Required gates per action type
  • Tool-call gates — Approval before tool execution
  • Audit logging — Record all approval decisions
  • Fail-closed behavior — Deny when approval unclear
  • Reviewer-safe evidence capture — Synthetic approval records
6. How should approval-bypass findings be documented safely?

A safe finding should include:

  • Approval gate — What decision point was tested?
  • Expected human decision point — Who should approve?
  • Observed behavior — What did the workflow do?
  • Evidence — Synthetic evidence of the finding
  • Uncertainty — What remains unknown?
  • Impact — Why does this matter?
  • Remediation — What should be fixed?

Avoid: Bypassing real approvals, manipulating production approval state, or providing operational bypass steps.

⚠️ High-Risk Anti-Pattern

A dangerous pattern is treating implied approval, model confidence, or ambiguous chat language as permission to execute a sensitive action.

❌ Unsafe Pattern

  • Implied approval
  • Stale approval context
  • Unclear human decision point
  • Live policy override
  • Workflow state manipulation
  • Direct tool execution
  • Unsupported authorization claims

✅ Safe Alternative

  • Explicit approval state
  • Documented decision point
  • No live execution
  • No workflow mutation
  • Control-mapped evidence
  • Fail-closed remediation
