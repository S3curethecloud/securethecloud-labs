# Senior Consultant Enterprise Agent Delivery Capstone — Architecture Note

## Purpose

This capstone integrates the complete Enterprise Agent Developer L2 Track into one synthetic client-engagement package.

The learner must connect:

- discovery and stakeholder analysis
- current-state process mapping
- use-case prioritization and value framing
- task-oriented and conversational agent design
- enterprise RAG and tool-use architecture
- deterministic routing and multi-agent orchestration
- gateway, MCP, registry, and API contracts
- OPA policy and Responsible AI controls
- OpenTelemetry, SLO, cost, and performance engineering
- cloud delivery, containers, CI/CD, and platform collaboration
- implementation planning, executive communication, and team mentorship

The governing model is:

```text
Discover before designing
Define measurable outcomes
Map authority and risk boundaries
Select architecture from requirements
Plan delivery in controlled increments
Preserve evidence for every decision
Communicate tradeoffs by audience
Assign accountable owners
Do not claim production readiness without production evidence
```

## Synthetic Engagement Context

The capstone uses a fictional multinational enterprise that wants an employee-service agent to answer policy questions, retrieve approved internal knowledge, create controlled service requests, and escalate sensitive cases.

The organization has:

- fragmented HR and IT service processes
- multiple regional data-residency requirements
- AWS and Microsoft cloud estates
- existing APIs with inconsistent contracts
- limited AI operating maturity
- security, legal, compliance, platform, data, and business stakeholders
- a requirement for measurable business value and audit-ready evidence

No real client, employee, system, credential, record, or production environment is used.

## Readiness Decision

The learner must issue one of three synthetic recommendations:

```text
GO
Required evidence is complete and risks are within the approved synthetic threshold.

CONDITIONAL GO
Specific controls, dependencies, or evidence must be completed before the next stage.

NO-GO
The design has unresolved authority, data, safety, integration, operational, or evidence gaps.
```

A recommendation must never be based on presentation quality alone. It must be grounded in traceable requirements, test plans, controls, ownership, and evidence.

## Core Control Rules

1. Discovery findings must be separated from assumptions.
2. Business outcomes must have measurable indicators and owners.
3. MVP scope must exclude workflows whose authority or evidence is unresolved.
4. Architecture decisions must record alternatives and tradeoffs.
5. Agent autonomy must never exceed deterministic policy and approval boundaries.
6. RAG sources, tools, APIs, and MCP capabilities must have explicit authority contracts.
7. Governance controls must map to enforcement points and evidence owners.
8. Delivery plans must define acceptance, rollback, and operational ownership.
9. Executive updates must distinguish facts, risks, assumptions, and decisions required.
10. Mentorship must produce reusable quality standards rather than undocumented individual knowledge.
11. Synthetic evidence must not be represented as client, production, or operational proof.
12. Track completion proves curriculum implementation, not real-world deployment.

## Safety Model

The capstone uses fictional stakeholders, synthetic process maps, mock architecture decisions, illustrative delivery plans, sample governance evidence, hypothetical executive updates, and design-only mentorship artifacts.

No stakeholder is contacted. No client workshop is run. No production decision is made. No model, agent, retrieval system, tool, API, policy engine, telemetry pipeline, CI/CD pipeline, cloud platform, credential, customer record, or production environment is executed or accessed.

The LAB makes no claim that the synthetic engagement, controls, architecture, delivery plan, or readiness decision has been validated in a real client or production environment.
